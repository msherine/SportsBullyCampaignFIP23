It's Not Football
But It's Still There

While we say physical abuse is concerning in Football because it’s aggressive. Swimming isn't immune.

What Is
“Bullying In Swimming”?

Swimming, despite being a physically and mentally challenging sport, is often considered lesser-known compared to more popular sports. Swimmers undergo aggressive training to enhance their performance and increase their chances of winning, but this can result in physical and emotional burnout. Emotional bullying is another issue in swimming, which can be detrimental to the mental health and overall performance of swimmers. Swimmers may also experience discrimination based on gender, race, or sexual orientation. It is crucial for the swimming community to recognize and prevent these challenges by promoting a positive and inclusive team culture, educating athletes and coaches, and creating safe spaces for swimmers.

Physical Bullying

Hitting, slapping, throwing swimming equipment like towel snapping, tripping, head butting and physical violence against another’s will

Verbal Bullying

Name-calling, nasty and cruel nicknames, taunting, rudeness and threats of violence and/or harm to a teammate or another student.

Social Bullying

Ganging up, preventing a person from going somewhere, taking a person’s possessions gossiping, embarrassment of a player in front of others.

Why Does It Need
More Attention?

Stopping Toxic Culture

Sports should promote and foster an environment in which athletes feel safe and secure.

Protecting Rights

Swimmers have the right to train and compete in a harassment- and discrimination-free environment.

Promoting Positive Actions

Kindness, acceptance, and inclusiveness should be encouraged.

Building Healthy Social Relationship

Bullying in sports can make it harder for players to grow.
